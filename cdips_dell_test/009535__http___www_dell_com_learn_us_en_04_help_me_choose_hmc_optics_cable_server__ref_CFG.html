Note: If you require connectivity at a distance further than 50 meters, you cannot select a direct attach cable. However, most direct attach cable options are up to 7 meters. For long distances, you must select the right combination of optics and cables based on distance and speed. This should be used a guide to help you make your selection. However, not all optics and cables work with all Dell Networking switches. Contact your Dell representative if you questions.
Optics 
 
Optics are small form-factor pluggables (SFP, SFP+, QSFP+) that allow you to customize your connectivity, support a chosen distance between the two devices, and select custom cables.
Cables 
 Cables come in various lengths and can be used with a wide range of short and long range optics. To use cables, you must select appropriate optics for connecting both ends of the cable.
Direct Attach Cables 
 Direct attach cables do not require separate optics as the optics and cables are integrated. Available in a variety of short lengths up to 7 meters. 
Breakout Cables 
 Transform one larger connection into multiple smaller connections. For instance, you can breakout a 40Gbps connection into 4x 10Gbps connections. These cables are perfect for expanding slower speed port counts where density is necessary. Breakout cables are available as standard cables requiring optics and are also available as direct attach cables. Start by choosing the appropriate tab above.

Optic Type
SFPSmall form factor pluggable supporting data rates up to 1,000Mbps (1Gbps)
SFP+Standardized form factor which offers a smaller footprint and lower power consumption than XFP transceivers. Supports data rates up to 10,000Mbps (10Gbps)
QSFP+Quad (4-channel) Small Form-factor Pluggable supporting data rates up to 40Gbps
Connections
CX44-lane copper standard that works up to 15m and offers low power, low cost and low latency, but has a bigger form factor and more bulky cables than the single lane SFP+
RJ45Registered Jack number 45. These are commonly used at the end of Cat5 and Cat6 cables
Cables
Cat 5Category 5 cable twisted pair cable for carrying signals. Can be used for 10/100/1000Mbps
Cat 6Category 6 cable. Can be used for 10Gbps
MMFMulti-mode fiber optic cable
SMFSingle-mode fiber optic cable
OM1Optical multi-mode performance 1 fiber optic cable
OM2Optical multi-mode performance 2 fiber optic cable
OM3Optical multi-mode performance 3 fiber optic cable
OM4Optical multi-mode performance 4 fiber optic cable
MTPMulti-fiber Termination Push-on
Breakout CableOptical fiber cable containing several jacketed simplex optical fibers. Generally is used with one 40Gbps connection broken into four 10Gbps connections
Direct Attach CableConnects from one SFP+ slot to another, without the need for SFP+ transceivers
 
Peripheral Type Key Facts 
SFP SX Optic, 1GbE
 

Use Case: Generally for 1GbE connectivity longer than 100m where Cat5 copper stops working, between floors in a building or two close buildings for instance.
Ethernet Standard: 1000BASE-SX
Connection: LC Multimode Fiber
Wavelength: 850nm
550 meter max distance over OM2 Multimode Fiber
220 meter max distance over OM1 Multimode Fiber 

SFP LX Optic, 1GbE 
Use Case: For 1GbE connectivity longer than 550m, between buildings or for much longer distances, up to 10km
Ethernet Standard: 1000BASE-LX
Connection: LC Single-Mode Fiber
Wavelength: 1310nm
10km max distance over Single-Mode Fiber 
SFP FX Optic, 100mb

 

Use Case: For long distance 100mb ("Fast Ethernet") connectivity, up to 2km
Ethernet Standard: 1000BASE-FX
Connection: LC Multimode Fiber
Wavelength: 1310nm
2km max distance over OM1 or OM2 Multimode Fiber

SFP ZX Optic, 1GbE

 		Use Case: For extremely long distance 1GbE connectivity, 80km reach.
Ethernet Standard: 1000BASE-ZX (unoffical but widely accepted standard)
Connection: LC Single-Mode Fiber
Wavelength: 1550nm
80km max distance over 9/125um Single-Mode Fiber
SFP 1000Base-T, 1GbE 

Use Case: Converts SFP or SFP+ port into a 1GbE RJ45 port, for standard Cat5/Cat6 copper ethernet cable connectivity
Ethernet Standard: 1000BASE-T
Connection: RJ45 CAT5/CAT6/etc copper ethernet cable (the same your laptop docking station uses)
100m max distance
Note: some switches allow autonegotiation to 10/100 with this transceiver as well 

SFP+ SR Optic, 10GbE
 

Use Case: For 10GbE SFP+ port connectivity longer than 7m (7m and shorter generally will use cheaper SFP+ Direct Attach Cables (Twinax), but this optic is still an option for short distances)
Ethernet Standard: 10GBASE-SR
Connection: LC Multimode Fiber
Wavelength: 850nm
300 meters max distance over OM3 fiber (in other words, use aqua "OM3 LC/LC fiber cables")
400 meters max distance over OM4 fiber
82 meters max distance over OM2 fiber (orange fiber)
33 meters max distance over OM1 fiber
Note: the LRM optic (below) maxes out at 220m, but it can do this on OM1 and OM2 fiber as well--if your customer is already wired for OM1/2, LRM may be the right choice for distances over 33m/82m respectively 

SFP+ LR Optic, 10GbE
 		Use Case: For 10GbE SFP+ port connectivity longer than 300m, and very long distance connections (up to 10km)
Ethernet Standard: 10GBASE-LR
Connection: LC Single-Mode Fiber
Wavelength: 1310nm
10km max distance over Single-Mode Fiber 
SFP+ LRM Optic, 10GbE 

Use Case: For 10GbE SFP+ port connectivity between 7m and 220m where OM1/OM2 fiber infrastructure is in use (compare to SR optic above)
Ethernet Standard: 10GBASE-LRM
Connection: LC Multimode Fiber
Wavelength: 1310
220m max distance on OM1, OM2, and OM3 fibre cables.

 

 
SFP+ ER Optic, 10GbE

 		Use Case: "Extended Reach" - For very long distance 10GbE SFP+ port connectivity up to 40km
Ethernet Standard: 10GBASE-ER
Connection: LC Single-Mode Fiber
Wavelength: 1550nm
40km max distance over engineered links, 30km over standard links 
QSFP+ SR Optic, 40GbE 
Use Case: For QSFP+ port 40GbE or 4x 10GbE (breakout) connectivity over 7m
Ethernet Standard: 40GBASE-SR4
Connection: MTP Multimode Fiber
100m max distance over OM3 MTP Multimode Fiber
150m max distance over OM4 MTP Multimode Fiber (engineered cable link)
Note: You can use MTP to MTP for 40GbE to 40GbE connectivity, or you can use MTP to 4xLC, for a breakout to 4x SFP+ 10GbE connections.
When breakout mode is enabled on the QSFP+ port, the switch CLI sees all 4x 10GbE ports as separate port numbers, managed normally. 
QSFP+ ESR Optic, 40GbE

 		Use Case: For QSFP+ port 40GbE or 4x 10GbE (breakout) connectivity between 125m and 400m
Ethernet Standard: 40GBASE-ESR
Connection: MTP Multimode Fiber
300m max distance over OM3 MTP Multimode Fiber
400m max distance over OM4 MTP Multimode Fiber
Note: You can use MTP to MTP for 40GbE to 40GbE connectivity, or you can use MTP to 4xLC, for a breakout to 4x SFP+ 10GbE connections.
When breakout mode is enabled on the QSFP+ port, the switch CLI sees all 4x 10GbE ports as separate port numbers, managed normally. 
QSFP+ LR4 Optic, 40GbE 
Use Case: For QSFP+ port 40GbE long distance connectivity up to 10km
Ethernet Standard: 40GBASE-LR4
Connection: Single Mode Fiber, LC connection
Note: This optic will NOT physically break out to 4x 10GbE connections. However, from a software/logical perspective,
the ports on either side can be set into breakout mode--as long as you're going from one QSFP+ port to one QSFP+ port physically.
